Internet Access in Low-Income Countries


Fixed broadband subscriptions is an important measure used to track how internet access has changed across the globe. It refers to fixed subscriptions to high-speed access to the public Internet (specifically those at downstream speeds greater than or equal to 256 kilobits per second). This includes cable modem, DSL, fiber-to-the-home/building, other fixed (wired)-broadband subscriptions, satellite broadband and terrestrial fixed wireless broadband. It excludes subscriptions that have access to data communications (including the Internet) via mobile-cellular networks. It includes both residential subscriptions and subscriptions for organizations (Source: World Bank).

Developing/low-income regions in the world have seen increases over time, however growth has been gradual and variable. For instance, in 2020 the East Asia & Pacific and Latin America & Caribbean regions recorded 25.33 subscriptions per 100 people and 14.83 subscriptions per 100 people respectively. However, Eastern & Southern Africa and Western & Central Africa recorded 0.74 subscriptions per 100 people and 0.39 subscriptions per 100 people respectively.



How has internet access changed over time in relation to internet cost?



Internet access increases as cost of internet decreases. As the circles grow larger (indicating increased access), they tend to be greener (indicating decreased cost). In areas with high proportions of low-income countries, such as Africa and the Americas, we still find relatively high internet cost, while high-income areas, such as in Europe, have low cost of internet. Interestingly, CIS countries (Azerbaijan, Armenia, Belarus, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Moldova, Russia, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan, Uzbekistan and Ukraine) have some of the lowest cost of internet in the world, and also have fairly high access, and accordingly fairly high access, although some countries with higher cost of internet have higher levels of access. Thus, it is important to note that the regional government and culture also play a role in internet access in a particular nation (Source: International Telecommunication Union).



How has global educational attainment changed over the same time period as internet access has increased?

Education attainment worldwide has improved from 1995 to 2020. The percentage of children who are not enrolled in primary school have decreased from 27.42% in 1995 to 21.35% in 2020 worldwide. The percentage of teenagers who have enrolled in secondary school also improved from 56.64% in 1995 to 65.1% in 2020.



How have patent applications changed over the same time period as internet access has increased?

Access to internet is correlated with increased innovation. Residents have filed more patents over time as broadband subscriptions have increased worldwide over the past 2 decades. This may be due to increasing internet access allowing for the growth of technology and development in countries, thereby leading to increased innovation.
